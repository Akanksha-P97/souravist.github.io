My Journey With Design

October 02, 2019 / by Sourav Kumar Sahoo

I take it a privilege to share with you a snippet of my walk through the designs. It has been nearly two years since I had got fascinated by the aesthetics, colors, lines, shapes and the infinite possibilities they could weave together. I’d keep it very simple, as I’m just a learner like you and not an expert at all by any means. All I could share is just my story and perception towards the things. Please don’t see this as any exhaustive guideline, rather take inspirations from it and write your own story.

It all started from Quora, where I got mesmerized with the minimalist designs carried out by one of the user Named Sahil Dev. You may have a look through his answers. After having a glance through his works, somewhere deep inside I realized, how would it look, if I give it a try! Prior to that, I had very little experience with Photoshop back in school days where my job was limited to a very basic level of functionalities.

Well! For the first time I got introduced to a new tool titled “ADOBE ILLUSTRATOR”. After using that, I’ve never looked back! Just out of curiosity, I threw myself the challenge on the date December 10, 2017 to work consistently for next 100 days on making new designs absolutely every day without a single pause irrespective of whether it’s a Sunday or my exam day! Just to keep a track of my growth, I used to send them to my friends and take their suggestions for improvements. Believe me, nothing has helped me to grow more than this!

To be precise, on the 62nd Day I got selected for an internship in a startup based in Haryana for designing posters and banners for their websites, promotions and newspaper printings. I continued it for one month according to the agreement and then the term got extended for more five months.

Fast forwarding the story, I had the privilege to design the t-shirts for AAVAHAN 2019 & INOVOATE 3.0 which were fortunate enough to be accepted by the SUIIT community. Later on, I ended up with an in-office internship last summer in a Gurgaon based start-up, where I had worked on the frontend development of a major ongoing project.

With experiences, you would understand the field of designs is not about aesthetics or colors only. Rather it’s a tool to address human problems and give voices where words fail! So, pick this tool with a sincere devotion and you’d be surprised to see the wonders it can do.
