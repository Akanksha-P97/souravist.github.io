A Trip to Sriharikota: India's Space Odyssey!

October 15, 2019 / by Sourav Kumar Sahoo

“Twenty years from now you’ll be more disappointed by the things you didn’t do than by the ones you did do. So throw off the bowlines. Sail away from the safe harbor. Catch the trade winds in your sails. Explore. Dream, Discover.”

The above lines are often credited to Mark Twain, whereas some identify H Jackson Brown Jr for its inception. But, anyhow these precious integration of words served as a navigator few months back for the then B.Tech third Year batch (2016-2020) of SUIIT,when we buckled our shoes up and left the safety nests of the hostel rooms and went on a tour to the heart of Indian Space Mission: Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota, the satellite launching centre of the premier Indian Space Agency ISRO. Having the privilege to keep our feet on a place like this is none other than once in a lifetime kind of opportunity for most of us!

Rockets Models installed at the entrance of SDSC-SHAR

JANUARY 7, 2019

This day and the timeless memories associated with it would be cherished by us for the years to come. A group of 40 students, 4 teachers, an instructor from ISRO and the bus driver to assist us, witnessed the historic moments of the visit by the first ever group from Odisha to arrive at this highly secure satellite launching site.


Visiting one of the ISRO centres on the Golden Jubilee Year (1969-2019) is not less than a dream came true moment for most of us. Even many of us neither had such higher dreams ever! Sometimes it seems like, with the mask of ISRO, the nature chose us to give us an exposure to something much larger than our current set of strengths and standards to make us move towards contributing for a better future of this nation.


SULLURUPETA: THE GATEWAY TO SRIHARIKOTA!

The journey started with a bus trip from the Sullurupeta railway station to Sriharikota island. Security checking at every minimum distance was the first impression of this place that got registered in our minds. Aadhaar card and college ID along with formal attires had literally turned lifelines for us in that particular day. With a bit refreshment at the Aryabhatta guest house present inside the campus followed by the breakfast, we got ready to explore the Space research centre along with the instructor.

Sullurupeta Railway Station | Clicked By Sourav Kumar Sahoo


The presence of this centre over an island on the Bay of Bengal with the Pullicat lake nearby makes it a lucrative spot for the travellers. Along with that, the beauty lies inside the campus which is purely enriched with green lush of flora glorifying the Space Mission of India. Probably, that’s why the nearby town Sullurupeta boasts itself for serving as the Gateway to Sriharikota, the path to India’s Space Odyssey!

Main Entrance of SDSC-SHAR

It involves a whole lots of tough processes to have the permission granted from the ISRO authority to traverse through the SDSC-SHAR. Due to its nature of carrying out secret missions throughout the year, they can’t afford the interference of trespassers in their pursuit of social welfare.So only through having a strong network with any ISRO insiders, access can be granted to this ultrasecure zone.


In spite of all the careful efforts, we were running one hour late from the schedule which was supposed to be 9.30 AM. But, somehow by 10.40 AM we managed to be inside an auditorium to watch some introductory videos on SDSC-SHAR to recall the contributions of great minds like Dr. Vikram Sarabhai who’s widely regarded as the Father of Indian Space Programme and Prof. Satish Dhawan after whom this centre is named, before stepping into the ground zero to understand the activities carried out in the space missions. Prior to that, we had to leave our smart gadgets and other belongings behind and had to move on with an ID card a diary in our hands.

The Architectural Beauty of Sriharikota| Clicked by Sourav Kumar Sahoo

MISSION CONTROL CENTRE

Then we headed to the Mission Control Centre popularly called as MCC. The circular structured centre, inaugurated back in 2012 by then Hon’ble President of India Smt. Pratibha Patil, hosts some large display screens to showcase real time satellite data and a set of computers with chairs meant to be occupied by the top level scientists during the launching days and a VVIP audience gallery behind which had hosted the Hon’ble Prime Minister of India in the past. We were standing somewhere between the both. Ashok Kumar Meher, serving as the technical officer at ISRO, was constantly guiding us throughout the visit by explaining us each and every single thing we come across and putting his meticulous efforts to quench the thirst of the curious questions in our minds.


I personally got captivated with the name plates displaying Mission Director & Vehicle Director present over two of the monitors inside the centre. These two persons are ideally referred as the ultimate bosses of a space mission. During the T-15 Mins (15 Mins. Prior to the launch), if there occurs any criticalities with the rocket, only these two guys can take the call whether to go for the launch or make it delayed. Probably it would be the dreams of many young aspiring Indians to get seated someday inside the Mission Control Centre and we were fortunate enough to spend nearly half an hour there! What more beautiful things we could expect than this one in our engineering career!


LAUNCHPADS

Then the bus took us near the Launchpad I. As a rocket was under the development stage on the day of our visit, we were allowed to observe it from a distant place. This one is regarded as the oldest and the most reliable Launchpad of ISRO which has India’s successful Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM) launched back in Nov 2013 to its credit along with numerous successful missions. Just standing at that place and observing the assembling process of the rockets was a quite fulfilling personal experience for me. I was overwhelmed with the patriotism from top to bottom along with an urge to dedicate rest of my life for the nation. I’ve got all the highest sense of respect for those countless unsung heroes who pull the socks and work round the clock in the service of the nation.


Followed by this, Launchpad II turned out to be our next destination. Although having seen the videos of several launches from here on the television screens prior to the visit, I literally got blown away to see the majestic structure installed by the ISRO. The nearly 80M tall building known as umbilical tower is used for fuelling & launching of the rocket after getting assembled at a building present exactly 1 KM away from that place. The structure is much larger and dynamic than it appears on the photographs. One must visit here once to perceive this fact.


This Launchpad is credited with the successful launch of India’s Moon Mission Chandrayan II in 2019 along with many more other successful launches. As there was no space mission under progress, on the day of our visit, we had the scope to move to an extreme closer area of the launchpad which certainly gets unmanned in the launching days. We could clearly boast of once being at that place, where lies the path to break through the limitations of sky and get into the space! The memories of this still gives me goosebumps!


The Technical Setup inside the campus of SDSC-SHAR

The rest of the journey includes the observation of the powerful antennas installed to capture satellite data, checking a scalable model of the rocket and spending few minutes in the space museum singing the glittering past of this premier space agency. The trip got concluded with a meal in the canteen and few clicks at the main gate of SDSC-SHAR where photography is permitted. Meanwhile I had ensured every details of this tour get entered into my diary for the reference in future. In addition to that, I had politely collected the autographs of every single person on this trip including the instructor from ISRO to freeze these moments for the years to come. Probably, a billion dollar couldn’t even exchange the experience that ISRO has given us in that one single day! If life permits, I’d not hesitate ever to be in that place again!

The Students of SUIIT At Space Museum of SDSC-SHAR

In the end, I’d like to sum up with a sense of satisfaction deep inside for being in that place which had seen the footfalls of legendary scientists in the past who had dedicated their whole lives for the beautiful days we are enjoying today. Without any doubt, all the credits go to ISRO officials and the SUIIT authority for making this trip turn into a reality. Trips would also happen to us in the future, but the adventurous experience like this one would reverberate in our ears for the years to come!
